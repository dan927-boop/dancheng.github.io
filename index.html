About

Welcome! I am a postdoctoral scholar and an incoming neuroradiology fellow at UAB, where I combine my passion for neuroimaging with cutting-edge AI technology. My journey spans from Beijing to Seattle, and now Birmingham, each step deepening my commitment to improving how we diagnose and treat brain diseases.

My research focuses on developing deep learning tools for brain tumor classification and using vessel wall MRI to prevent strokes. This work has been published in leading journals including Clinical Cancer Research, Neuro-Oncology, and Radiology: Artificial Intelligence. I'm currently supported by the Ron and Christine Lee Raccoon Stroke Research Fellowship as I continue exploring new frontiers in cerebrovascular imaging.

Beyond research, I'm driven by a simple goal: translating complex imaging innovations into real benefits for patients. Whether through academic collaboration or science communication, I believe in making medical imaging more accessible and impactful.

Deep Learning for Brain Tumor Classification

Developing fully automated deep learning models to predict molecular subtypes of posterior fossa ependymomas, enabling non-invasive tumor characterization for improved treatment planning.

  • Published in Clinical Cancer Research (2024)
  • Fully automated pipeline from MRI to prediction
  • High clinical applicability potential

Vessel Wall MRI for Stroke Prevention

Utilizing high-resolution vessel wall MRI to characterize intracranial atherosclerotic plaques, identifying high-risk features for stroke prediction and prevention strategies.

  • Plaque characterization and risk stratification
  • Multi-center research collaboration
  • Integration with clinical stroke prevention
